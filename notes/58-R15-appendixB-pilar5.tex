This paper is proved by hand throughout, \emph{with a single exception}:
Theorem~\ref{thm:sandwich-bang} (the covering-constant identity
$C_\Delta(\tau_0)=1$ at $\tau_0=(\tfrac{13}{25},\tfrac12,\tfrac12)$) reduces, by
the human Lemmas~\ref{lem:extreme}--\ref{lem:termarg}, to the emptiness of an
explicit finite bisection tree $T(\tau_0)$, which is checked by an exact
rational computation. That computation is \emph{exact} (only
\texttt{fractions.Fraction}; no floating point, no $\varepsilon$; independent of
hardware and rounding) but not human-scale ($\approx1.6\times10^6$ nodes); we
therefore label Theorem~\ref{thm:sandwich-bang} \textbf{[computer-assisted]}.
Its validity does not rest on trusting the search: it rests on an independent
\emph{certificate checker} (below) that shares no code with the searcher and
re-derives every box and every pruning step. All other theorems, propositions,
and lemmas are proved in full in the text or in Appendix~\ref{app:tilings}. The
ancillary scripts elsewhere in this appendix remain \emph{secondary}
verification of hand proofs.

\subsection*{The certified computation (search / certificate / checker)}

Following the search-versus-verification separation of de Bruijn, the
emptiness of $T(\tau_0)$ is established in three artifacts:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Searcher} \texttt{c3\_balanced\_bb.py}: grows $T(\tau_0)$ by
depth-first bisection, pruning by P1--P4 (Lemma~\ref{lem:prunes}); it may use
any heuristics (it does not affect soundness). Emits a \emph{certificate}.
\item \textbf{Certificate} \texttt{c3\_sandwich\_certificate.txt}: a
deterministic preorder serialization of $T(\tau_0)$ storing only the tree shape
--- for each internal node the split coordinate and the exact rational split
point, and for each leaf the pruning rule and its exact witness (the index for
P2/P4, the uncovered edge for P3, or the positive-area sign cell for P3). It
does \emph{not} store the boxes.
\item \textbf{Checker} \texttt{bb\_certificate\_check.py} ($\approx$ one page,
imports nothing from the searcher): reconstructs every box from the tree shape
alone (so an arithmetic error in the searcher would surface here), and verifies
(i) \emph{tiling} --- the leaves partition $[0,1]^6$: the stream is the preorder
of a binary tree whose root is the cube and each internal node splits one
coordinate at a strictly interior point, the whole stream is consumed, and the
reconstruction stack ends empty (with the binary-tree identity
$\#\text{leaves}=\#\text{internal}+1$); and (ii) \emph{prunes} --- each leaf is
re-validated against its recorded rule and witness by an independent
re-implementation of the triangle geometry, of $w_0(\tau)$, of exact polygon
clipping, and of the four prune predicates. It prints \texttt{CERTIFICATE VALID}
only if all checks pass.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Input:} $\tau_0=(\tfrac{13}{25},\tfrac12,\tfrac12)$; threshold
$w_0(\tau_0)=\tfrac2{25}$ (recomputed independently by the checker and matched
against the certificate header). \textbf{Output:} $T(\tau_0)$ has $812{,}650$
internal nodes and $812{,}651$ leaves, all pruned; the checker reports
\texttt{CERTIFICATE VALID}. Leaves by rule:
\[
\underbrace{286{,}024}_{\mathrm{P1}\ (\sum r>1)}\quad
\underbrace{53{,}907}_{\mathrm{P2}\ (\text{extreme})}\quad
\underbrace{428{,}672}_{\mathrm{P3,\ edge}}\quad
\underbrace{2{,}354}_{\mathrm{P3,\ cell}}\quad
\underbrace{41{,}694}_{\mathrm{P4}\ (\text{empty})}.
\]
\textbf{Dependency:} the certified computation discharges \emph{only} case
(iii) (proper balanced coverings) of Theorem~\ref{thm:sandwich-bang}; cases (i)
and (ii) are the human Lemmas~\ref{lem:extreme} and~\ref{thm:twodir}. Within the
tree, the P2 leaves invoke Lemma~\ref{lem:extreme} and the P4 leaves invoke
Theorem~\ref{thm:twodir} (both proved by hand), so the genuinely computational
content is the P1 and P3 leaves ($717{,}050$ in all); of the P3 leaves, all but
$2{,}354$ are discharged by a one-dimensional edge computation
(Lemma~\ref{lem:edgered}), only $2{,}354$ needing the two-dimensional
positive-area cell test.

\subsection*{Independent corroboration (second tree)}

Beyond the certificate/checker, the emptiness was reproduced by a second search
on the \emph{relabelled} triple $(\tfrac12,\tfrac12,\tfrac{13}{25})$ (a cyclic
relabelling of $\tau_0$, the same covering problem) with a non-dyadic split
ratio $\tfrac5{11}$: this produces a \emph{different} tree ($1{,}040{,}919$
nodes versus $1{,}625{,}301$; different per-rule counts) with the same
\texttt{QUEUE EMPTY} verdict. Two structurally different trees agreeing is
corroboration; the authority for the theorem is nonetheless the independent
checker, which alone does not share the search logic (a common-mode bug in the
prune code would survive the two-tree test but not the checker's independent
re-implementation).
